2. Extending to Two Dimensions: The Buffon-Laplace Problem

The Buffon-Laplace Needle Problem generalises the original version by replacing the parallel lines with a rectangular grid: horizontal lines spaced apart and vertical lines spaced apart.

A needle of length (with and ) is dropped randomly. We now ask: what is the probability that the needle crosses at least one grid line?

This version was first attempted by Buffon in 1777, but his derivation contained an error. A correct solution was later published by Pierre-Simon Laplace in 1812, which is why the problem carries both names.
